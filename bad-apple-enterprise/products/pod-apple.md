# Pod Apple — The Launch Vehicle
**POD + Apple. Print-on-demand + Bad Apple brand.**

---

## What Pod Apple Is

Pod Apple is the print-on-demand product line that powers Phase 1 of all physical collections.

Every Chakka, Quasheba, and Corefuel garment launches through Pod Apple first:
- **Zero inventory risk** — products only printed when ordered
- **Real demand signal** — know what sells before committing capital
- **No warehouse needed** — ships direct from POD supplier to customer
- **Fast to test** — new design live within days, not months

When a SKU hits **50+ units sold** → it graduates to in-house production:
- Better margins (cost per unit drops significantly)
- NFC/QR authentication tags stitched in (not available on POD)
- Full quality control in-house
- Packaging upgrades (custom boxes, tissue, thank-you inserts)

**Pod Apple = the seed. In-house = the tree.**

---

## The Graduation Mechanic

| Stage | Production | Margin | Authentication | Packaging |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seed (0–49 units) | POD via Pod Apple | ~30–40% | None | Standard |
| Sprout (50–99 units) | Consider transition | — | Evaluate | Upgrade pending |
| Tree (100+ units) | In-house | ~55–65% | NFC/QR tag stitched | Custom Bad Apple |

The 50-unit threshold is a guideline — not a hard rule. Use it as the signal to evaluate
margin, reorder volume, and whether the design has legs.

## POD Supplier Considerations

When selecting a POD supplier for Pod Apple, evaluate:

| Criteria | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Print quality on dark fabrics | Chakka line is mostly dark colourways |
| Embroidery capability | Fibonacci leg embroidery on joggers requires it |
| Jamaican / Caribbean shipping | Diaspora customers in JA, UK, CA, US |
| Minimum order for bulk | Graduation transition point |
| White-label / custom neck label | Brand integrity — no supplier logo visible |
| Returns handling | Customer experience |

## Revenue Model

**Bot sale → member buys tee via Pod Apple link → Pod Apple fulfills**

Margin example (Chakka Tee):
- Retail price: $45
- POD cost (est.): ~$18–22
- Gross margin: ~$23–27 (51–60%)
- After shipping subsidy: ~$20–24 per unit

At 50 units: ~$1,000–1,200 gross profit from one tee SKU in Phase 1.
That funds the in-house transition for that SKU.
